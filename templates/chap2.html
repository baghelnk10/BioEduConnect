Tissues

Learning Objectives

This section would outline the goals of the learning module and what learners should be able to achieve by the end. It could also include a brief overview of the topics covered.

Learn More
×

    Students Should be able to-

  1. define tissue and differentiate it from the cell.
  2. differentiate between plant and animal tissue. You should be able to explain cell theory.
  3. identify and classify different types of plant tissues. You should be able to describe the structural organization of a cell.
  4. categorize different types of meristematic tissue based on their location and functions. You should be able to list the various components of a cell and state their functions.
  5. differentiate between simple permanent and complex permanent tissue. You should be able to differentiate between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.
  6. explain simple and permanent tissues, their specific features, structure, location, and function. You should be able to draw and label plant and animal cells.
  7. identify and classify different types of animal tissues. You should be able to have a basic understanding of chromosomes, DNA, and types of cell division.
  8. learn different types of epithelial tissues, their location, and function.
  9. identify, classify, and describe the function of different types of connective tissue.
  10. explain different types of muscular tissue based on their structure and function.
  11. explain nervous tissue and draw a neuron diagram.
  12. develop scientific vocabulary, critical thinking, and comprehension of text.
  13. develop creativity, reasoning, analytical skills, and accuracy of diagrams.
